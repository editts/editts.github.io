EdiTTS: User Editable Text-to-Speech

Jaesung Tae
Yale University Hyeongju Kim
Neosapience, Inc.
[arXiv] [Code]

Abstract

We present EdiTTS, a novel speech editing methodology based on score-based generative modeling for text-to-speech synthesis. EdiTTS allows targeted, granular editing of audio segments, both in terms of content and pitch. Compared to conventional speech editing methods, which employ simple concatenation or resampling-based algorithms, EdiTTS estimates scores to gradually denoise Gaussian priors into realistic mel-spectrograms. Specifically, we apply coarse yet deliberate perturbations in the Gaussian prior space to induce desired behavior from the diffusion model, while applying masks and softening kernels to ensure that edits are only applied to the target region. Listening tests demonstrate that EdiTTS is capable of reliably generating natural-sounding audio while satisfying user-imposed requirements.
